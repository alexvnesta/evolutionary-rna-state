# Bulk-RNA inference of clonal-evolutionary state as an ICB-response predictor

The proposal chains three inferences: reconstruct clonal-evolutionary trajectories from matched scRNA-seq and WXS, distil them into a small set of trajectory classes, then train a classifier that recovers the class from **bulk RNA alone** and test whether the class predicts immune-checkpoint-blockade (ICB) response. Each link has substantial prior art, but the assembled chain does not appear to have been published. That combination of well-supported components with an unpublished integration is the useful finding, and it also locates precisely where the statistical risk sits.

## Reconstructing clones and their expression states

The canonical tool for the specific "WXS clone tree plus scRNA cells" workflow is cardelino ([McCarthy 2020](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-020-0766-3)), which builds a guide clonal tree from exome data and then probabilistically assigns single-cell transcriptomes to clones using somatic variants recovered from the scRNA reads, explicitly modelling the extreme sparsity of variant coverage in single-cell data. Where copy number rather than point mutations defines the subclones, clonealign ([Campbell 2019](https://doi.org/10.1186/s13059-019-1645-z)) maps expression to CNV-defined clones. Where no matched DNA exists, Numbat ([Gao 2022](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41587-022-01468-y)) infers allele-specific copy number and a clonal phylogeny from the transcriptome itself using population haplotype phasing, and is currently the strongest of the reference-free callers. The frontier has moved to co-assay methods that read genotype and transcriptome in the same cell: GoT-Multi ([Pak 2025](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.xgen.2025.101036)) co-detects multiple somatic genotypes and whole transcriptomes in FFPE tissue and pairs them with a machine-learning genotyping pipeline, removing the probabilistic-assignment step that cardelino has to perform. The practical consequence is that link 1 is not a research risk. Clone-labelled single cells with a DNA-defined phylogeny are a solved, tooled output, and the DNA layer for turning WXS variant-allele frequencies into cancer-cell fractions and subclone clusters (PyClone, [Roth 2014](https://doi.org/10.1038/nmeth.2883); ABSOLUTE, [Carter 2012](https://doi.org/10.1038/nbt.2203)) is equally mature.

## The pseudotime trap

The one conceptual hazard to design around is that a pseudotime axis is not a clonal-evolution axis. Pseudotime orders cells by transcriptional-state similarity and was built for differentiation; a subclone can occupy several transcriptional states and a state can be shared across subclones, so a Monocle- or Slingshot-style ordering does not recover genetic ancestry. The methods above avoid this by anchoring expression to a DNA-defined tree rather than trusting expression similarity to reconstruct lineage, which is exactly why the trajectory *class* in this design must be a genetic-phylogeny descriptor (branching topology, truncal-versus-subclonal driver placement, clonal-versus-subclonal neoantigen load), not a transcriptional pseudotime.

## Turning trees into a small set of classes

Reducing per-patient phylogenies to a categorical typology is established. Reanalysing the 100-patient TRACERx NSCLC cohort ([Jamal-Hanjani 2017](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMoa1616288)), [Liao 2020](https://doi.org/10.1002/cam4.3541) grouped patients into three evolutionary subtypes from the branching structure of their trees, one branched and one dominated by early accumulation of variation, and reported survival differences across them. That is a working demonstration that "evolutionary pattern" can serve as a discrete, prognostic categorical variable. The complementary idea that evolutionary *trajectories recur across patients* is formalised by REVOLVER ([Caravagna 2018](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-018-0108-x)), which detects repeated evolutionary paths across a cohort of multi-region-sequenced tumours, and is the natural engine for defining a finite, reusable class library rather than hand-labelling topologies. Both precedents build the classes from multi-region DNA, not from RNA, which is the point at which the proposal departs from what has been done.

## The immunotherapy axis

The evolution-to-antigenicity-to-ICB axis is load-bearing, not speculative. [McGranahan 2016](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aaf1490) showed that a high burden of clonal (truncal) neoantigens correlates with improved survival, higher T-cell infiltration and durable ICB benefit in NSCLC and melanoma, that sensitivity to PD-1 and CTLA-4 blockade is enhanced in clonal-neoantigen-rich tumours, and that T cells recognising clonal neoantigens are detectable in durable responders, while subclonal neoantigen diversity blunts the effect. TRACERx supplies the escape arm: allele-specific HLA loss of heterozygosity is a pervasive, subclonally enriched immune-evasion mechanism ([McGranahan 2017](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2017.10.001)), and functional single-clone work now confirms subclonal immune evasion directly, with organoid-plus-TIL co-cultures derived from TRACERx tumours showing individual subclones that differ in their ability to escape T-cell killing ([Dijkstra 2025](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ccell.2025.06.012)). During therapy, neoantigen immunoediting is observable: in the CheckMate-153 biomarker cohort, early loss of mutations and neoantigens under nivolumab tracks with clinical benefit ([Alban 2024](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41591-024-03240-y)). So the label the proposal wants to predict, evolutionary state as a proxy for antigenicity and escape potential, is one the immunotherapy literature already treats as mechanistically real, which is what makes it worth predicting from a cheaper assay.

## Inferring heterogeneity from bulk: the real gap

This is where the proposal is genuinely under-explored, and the distinction between what exists and what is proposed is sharp. Bulk-derived heterogeneity measures are well established but almost entirely **DNA**-based, since ABSOLUTE and PyClone infer subclonal structure from somatic mutations and copy number rather than expression. Transcriptome-level heterogeneity from bulk does exist but returns a **scalar**, not a trajectory class: SpliceHetero ([Kim 2019](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0223520)) quantifies spliceomic intratumour heterogeneity from bulk RNA and relates it to stage, survival and subtype, and transcriptomic-ITH scores are in the same spirit. The closest methodological template to the proposed bridge is single-cell-to-bulk signature transfer: [Zhang 2022](https://doi.org/10.1186/s13073-022-01050-w) derived a stemness signature from ICB-treated scRNA-seq cohorts and showed it predicts ICB response across ten bulk transcriptomic cohorts, and purely expression-based ICB predictors such as IMPRES ([Auslander 2018](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41591-018-0157-9)) demonstrate that bulk RNA carries recoverable ICB-relevant signal. The template exists; what no one appears to have done is make the *transferred label a clonal-evolutionary trajectory class* rather than a pathway score or a response label. The public bulk ICB cohorts to test against are well characterised: melanoma anti-PD-1 ([Hugo 2016](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2016.02.065); [Riaz 2017](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2017.09.028)) and anti-CTLA-4 ([Van Allen 2015](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aad0095)).

## Verdict

The idea is sound and sits squarely on the project thesis, that early drivers set an evolutionary trajectory whose downstream RNA state governs antigenicity and ICB response. Three of the four links are solved or precedented: clone-resolved expression (cardelino, Numbat, GoT-Multi), evolutionary-pattern classes (Liao and REVOLVER on TRACERx), and the clonal-architecture-to-ICB mechanism (McGranahan, Dijkstra, Alban). The novel and load-bearing step is transferring a *categorical trajectory label* into bulk RNA, and the honest framing of its risk is this: bulk RNA is a clone mixture, so recovering a trajectory from it is underdetermined, and de-novo phylogeny reconstruction from bulk expression is not realistic. The defensible design is supervised. Treat the scRNA/WXS-derived trajectory class as a training label and ask only whether bulk RNA is a good-enough proxy to recover it, exactly as the stemness-signature and IMPRES precedents recover their labels. Framed that way it is a tractable classification problem with a clear novelty claim and existing test cohorts; framed as reconstruction it is not. The gating practical question is cohort size, meaning how many tumours with matched scRNA-seq **and** WXS **and** ICB outcome actually exist publicly, and that is the first thing worth quantifying before committing.
